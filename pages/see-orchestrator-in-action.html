How Orchestrator Works

You configure. Enroller executes.

Orchestrator is the layer behind Pylaex Enroller. Everything your agents see and every decision made during enrollment starts here — with choices your team makes, not an IT release cycle.

What you control

Screen order and layout. Which questions appear and when. Where to call external APIs and which ones. Underwriting logic — approve, decline, refer, reflexive. Product rules and rate tables. What data gets passed downstream to your PAS.

What you don't need

An engineering ticket. A release cycle. A developer on the call. A change management process that takes 90 days. An IT queue. A testing sprint. Orchestrator changes are live when you promote them — from sandbox to production in minutes.

What you can configure without touching code

Screen Order & Layout

Drag and drop screens to change the order agents see them. Add, remove, or reorder questions within any screen. Changes reflect in Enroller immediately after promotion.

Questions & Branching

Choose which questions appear and under what conditions. Set reflexive logic — if an applicant answers Yes to X, automatically show questions Y and Z. Carrier-controlled, no code required.

API Calls

Turn external data calls on or off per product, per state, or per eligibility type. Choose when to call an API, what to do with the result, and when to bypass it entirely.

Underwriting Logic

Configure approve, decline, refer, and table-rating outcomes based on any combination of applicant responses, API results, and product rules. All decisions are auditable and logged.

Product Rules

Rate tables, eligibility windows, benefit amounts, and coverage tiers — all configurable without a release cycle. Update a rate and promote it in minutes, not weeks.

Sandbox & Promotion

Every change happens in sandbox first. Validate, preview in Enroller, and promote to production when ready. No IT handoff. No change management process. Your team owns it.
