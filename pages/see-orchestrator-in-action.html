How Orchestrator works

You configure. Enroller executes.

Orchestrator is the layer behind Pylaex Enroller. Everything your agents see — every screen, every question, every API call — is defined here by your operations team, not an engineering sprint.

What you control

Screen order. Question order within screens. Which questions are active or suppressed. Where to call external APIs and when to bypass them. Underwriting logic — approve, decline, refer, reflexive triggers. Rate tables and eligibility rules.

What you don't need

A developer. An IT ticket. A release cycle. A change management process. A testing sprint. Configuration changes go from sandbox to live in minutes. Your business team owns the product.

What you can configure without touching code

Screen Order

Drag and drop to change the sequence agents see screens. Every reorder reflects immediately in Enroller after you promote.

Questions & Branching

Activate or suppress questions within any screen. Set reflexive logic — answering Yes to one question automatically surfaces follow-up questions.

API Calls

Toggle external data calls on or off by product, state, or eligibility type. Choose when to call, what to do with results, and when to bypass.

Underwriting Logic

Configure approve, decline, refer, and table-rating outcomes based on responses, API results, and carrier-defined product rules. Every decision logged.

Product Rules

Rate tables, eligibility windows, benefit amounts — all configurable without a release cycle. Update and promote in minutes.

Sandbox & Promotion

Every change happens in sandbox first. Preview in Enroller, validate, then promote to production. Your team owns the timeline.
