Where the Name Comes From

pylae — The gates.  ·  -ex — Force issuing outward.

Thermopylae isn't a story about strength. It's about making the other side's advantage irrelevant. Small and mid-size carriers don't need to outspend the incumbents. They need what those gates gave: the position, the capability, the platform that made the other side's scale advantage not matter.

Pylaex was founded by career insurance operators who understand the real constraints of underwriting, distribution, and operations. Our first client more than doubled sales year over year by removing friction at the point of sale through intelligent application workflows.

Leadership

The Team

MJ
Chief Executive Officer

Matt Johnson

Matt Johnson is a career insurance operator with deep experience spanning financial reporting, product strategy, and enterprise growth. He served as Chief Actuary and VP of Strategy & Platform Enablement at a mid-size Midwestern carrier, where he owned platform strategy, capital allocation, and board-level communications.

Prior to founding Pylaex, Matt spent years watching carriers lose market share not because of their people or their products, but because their technology couldn't keep up. At Pylaex, Matt brings an operator's perspective to every product and go-to-market decision — ensuring the platform is built around how carriers actually work.

BB
Chief Technology Officer

Bill Butters

Bill Butters is the lead developer and technical architect of Pylaex. With decades of experience solving carrier challenges through technology, Bill has built systems that sit at the intersection of underwriting, operations, and distribution.

At his prior carrier, Bill led the design and development of a modern buying and underwriting platform that became a material driver of growth — helping enable rapid iteration, improved quality of business, and dramatically higher sales volume. Bill brings a rare combination of deep technical expertise and real-world carrier experience, ensuring Pylaex Orchestrator is designed for speed, configurability, and production-grade reliability.
